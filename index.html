Act I · The legal method

Commercial reality first.
Legal architecture last.

A product label is not a legal conclusion. The work begins by separating the commercial offering into activities, identifying the role and factual surface of each activity, and testing only the obligations and exposure rules that those facts can support.

I

Reconstruct the target

Separate brand claims from verifiable product, customer, data, payment and governance facts.

II

Decompose the activity

Identify actor, input, action, output, affected party, autonomy and human involvement.

III

Apply the legal taxonomy

Resolve Behavior Class, Lane, Surface and terminal Subcategory without collapsing one dimension into another.

IV

Reconstruct the position

Convert triggered, controlled and evidence-limited findings into drafting, control, diligence and counsel routes.

What the system preservesPermitted evidence, factual conditions, explicit exclusions, forbidden inferences, safe fallbacks and the path from finding to response.

What remains legal judgmentApplicability, sufficiency, competing characterisations, negotiation posture, local-law consequence and final reliance.

03

Applied exposure tests

Each row is resolved against conditions, evidence, controls and exclusions—not intuition.

Conditions
  • AsterPay initiates or materially controls payment activity.
  • The regulated role is not transparently allocated.
Evidence applied

Licensed sponsor bank is identified; AsterPay presents an orchestration role and no-custody model.

Resolution

Controlled only if transaction control, customer disclosure and contractual allocation match the public model.

Conditions
  • Score materially influences applicant outcome.
  • Human reviewer lacks genuine authority or information.
Evidence applied

Scoring and manual review are visible, but the degree of independent human judgment is not.

Resolution

Route decision matrix, override evidence, reason codes and appeal process to qualified review.

Conditions
  • Applicant or transaction context reaches a third-party model.
  • Purpose, retention or downstream use is not adequately bounded.
Evidence applied

External routing is disclosed; purpose limitation and provider-side training treatment are not established.

Resolution

Require provider schedule, no-training or bounded-use position, deletion route, location and sub-processor chain.

Conditions
  • Terms govern material platform use.
  • Assent is not conspicuous or auditable.
Evidence applied

Terms exist, but the signup flow and versioned assent record are not demonstrated.

Resolution

Repair the acceptance flow, version capture and evidence trail; clause wording alone cannot cure formation.

Act III · The system as proof

Built to preserve legal method—not replace it.

The engine is an inspectable legal workflow: evidence first, classification second, exposure application third, and human legal judgment at the point of reliance.

The system may organise evidence.It may not invent facts that the public footprint cannot prove.
The system may apply locked derivation rules.It may not convert a classification signal into a final legal conclusion.
The system may route triggered issues.It may not decide sufficiency, negotiation position or local-law consequence.
The system may prepare review-ready work.Final reliance remains with qualified human and local counsel review.

Implementation spine

  • Runtime · Node.js, Express, ES modules
  • Contracts · Zod schemas and deterministic validators
  • Legal intelligence · YAML/JSON registries and package-specific taxonomies
  • Semantic support · bounded model-assisted evaluation
  • State · Firestore, Cloud Tasks and review checkpoints
  • Delivery · evidence-led report and Qualified Review handoff
